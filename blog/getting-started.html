Why I'm Starting to Write

After a decade of building things for other people, I'm finally building in public. Here's why writing is part of that — and what you can expect from this space.

For the last ten years, most of my work has lived inside company walls. Billions of records processed, pipelines that saved hundreds of thousands of dollars, teams rebuilt from scratch — none of it visible to the outside world. The work was real, but the story wasn't being told.

That's changing now.

Why write?

Since stepping away from the corporate track in early 2025, I've been building with more freedom than I've ever had. I shipped a full-stack app, dove deep into agentic AI systems, and started learning in public. But I realized something was missing: I wasn't capturing the thinking behind the building.

Writing forces clarity. When you have to explain something to someone else, you discover what you actually understand versus what you've just been pattern-matching on. That alone makes it worth doing.

What to expect here

This space will be a mix of things — technical and personal, polished and raw. Some posts you might see:

  • Deep-dives into things I'm building — architecture decisions, tradeoffs, what worked and what didn't
  • Notes on AI and agentic systems — what's real, what's hype, what I'm actually using
  • Career reflections — lessons from a decade in engineering that I wish someone had told me earlier
  • Learning logs — honest accounts of figuring things out in real time

I'm not trying to build an audience or become a thought leader. I'm trying to think more clearly, share what I'm learning, and connect with people who are working on interesting problems.

The format

I'm keeping this simple on purpose. No CMS, no build tools, no newsletter popups. Just HTML files, hand-written, hosted on GitHub Pages. If you want to know when I post something new, the best way is to check back here or connect with me on LinkedIn.

The best time to start writing was years ago. The second best time is now.

So here we go. Thanks for reading — and if anything here resonates, I'd love to hear from you.
